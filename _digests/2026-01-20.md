## Top 10 — News {#top-news}

1. [OpenAI partners with Cerebras](https://openai.com/index/cerebras-partnership)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">AI frontier | OpenAI Blog</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">A notable infra/inference partnership; watch for speed/cost implications.</div>

2. [Differential Transformer V2](https://huggingface.co/blog/microsoft/diff-attn-v2)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">AI frontier | Hugging Face Blog</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">An attention variant for long-context efficiency; a clear comparison point vs standard Transformers.</div>

3. [The Conversational Exam: A Scalable Assessment Design for the AI Era](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10691v1)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">Education / learning sciences / edtech | arXiv</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">An assessment format designed to stay meaningful when students have AI.</div>

4. [Open Responses: What you need to know](https://huggingface.co/blog/open-responses)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">AI frontier | Hugging Face Blog</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">A practical multi-model workflow for comparing and integrating LLM outputs.</div>

5. [Evaluating 21st-Century Competencies in Postsecondary Curricula with Large Language Models: Performance Benchmarking and Reasoning-Based Prompting Strategies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10983v1)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">Education / learning sciences / edtech | arXiv</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">Benchmarks LLMs for curricular competency evaluation and prompting strategies.</div>

6. [aiPlato: A Novel AI Tutoring and Step-wise Feedback System for Physics Homework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09965v1)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">Education / learning sciences / edtech | arXiv</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">A tutoring design emphasizing step-wise feedback (closer to how students learn).</div>

7. [Take Out Your Calculators: Estimating the Real Difficulty of Question Items with LLM Student Simulations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09953v1)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">Education / learning sciences / edtech | arXiv</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">Uses simulated &quot;LLM students&quot; to estimate item difficulty for assessment design.</div>

8. [AI Sycophancy: How Users Flag and Respond](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10467v1)
   <div class="digest-item-meta">CSS / AI &amp; society | arXiv</div>
   <div class="digest-item-why">Empirical signals for detecting/handling overly agreeable LLM behavior.</div>
